Water Usage

Source 1

Website URL https://news.mit.edu/2025/explained-generative-ai-environmental-impact-0117
Author Adam Zewe
Abstract According to MIT News, water consumption is a critical yet often overlooked component of artificial intelligence’s environmental impact. Data centers require substantial amounts of water to cool servers and prevent overheating, with usage levels closely tied to energy consumption. As AI technologies continue to expand, this demand for water is expected to increase, placing additional pressure on global freshwater resources. The article emphasizes that many users are unaware of this hidden cost, as digital technologies often appear intangible. This perspective highlights the importance of increasing transparency and awareness around the physical resource demands of AI systems.

Source 2

Website URL https://www.unesco.org/reports/wwdr/en/2024/s
Author UN World Water Development Report
Abstract The UN Water provides a broader context for understanding the impact of AI on global water resources. The report explains that freshwater is already heavily used by agriculture, industry, and growing populations, leaving limited availability for additional demands. The expansion of AI infrastructure adds further strain to these systems, particularly in regions already experiencing water scarcity. This perspective connects AI to larger global water challenges, emphasizing that technological growth must be considered within existing environmental limits. It underscores the urgency of integrating water conservation strategies into the development of AI systems to prevent worsening resource inequality.
