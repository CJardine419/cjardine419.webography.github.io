Microchips

Source 1

Website URL https://www.unep.org/news-and-stories/story/ai-has-environmental-problem-heres-what-world-can-do-about
Author UN Environment Progamee
Abstract The United Nations highlights the environmental impact of microchip production by emphasizing the immense resource demands required to create digital technologies. Microchips rely on rare earth elements and critical minerals, which are often extracted through environmentally harmful mining practices that damage ecosystems and expose workers to toxic conditions. The organization notes that producing a single computer can require hundreds of kilograms of raw materials, demonstrating the imbalance between technological output and environmental cost. This perspective frames microchip production as part of a larger global sustainability issue, where the benefits of technological advancement are closely tied to environmental degradation. Overall, the United Nations underscores the urgent need for more sustainable resource management and ethical production methods within the growing AI industry.

Source 2

Website URL https://news.mit.edu/2025/explained-generative-ai-environmental-impact-0117
Author Adam Zewe
Abstract Research published by MIT News explores the environmental cost of microchips by focusing on the energy-intensive processes required to manufacture advanced computing hardware. The article explains that graphics processing units (GPUs), which are essential for AI systems, require significantly more energy to produce than traditional processors due to their complexity. Additionally, the growing demand for AI technologies has led to a surge in microchip production, increasing both energy consumption and carbon emissions. This perspective emphasizes the scalability problem of AI, where increased efficiency in technology does not necessarily reduce environmental impact. Instead, the expansion of AI systems drives further resource use, raising concerns about long-term sustainability and the need for more energy-efficient innovations.
